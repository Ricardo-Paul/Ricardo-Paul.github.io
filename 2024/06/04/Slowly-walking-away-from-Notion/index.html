I almost don't use Notion these days

Ricardo Paul
On
June 4, 2024
note taking

A few years ago if you had asked me what’s the best way to take notes and build a second brain I would have answered, without a second thought - Notion. And I would have been right because not only was I sold on it for its ability to organize knowledge, it actually was working for me.

I had a workspace with tens of pages and nested pages. I did my best to keep everything organized and neat. I was happy. Except, at some point, it started not to fit my daily workflow the way I would prefer it to.

A big chunk of the notes I take daily is around the issue or project I’m currently working on, so writing them in a notes.md file which I git-ignore is a lot more convenient than having to look them up in Notion. So overtime I’ve gotten pretty used to doing a quick search on one big file. I like it for speed and convenience. While I was attracted to Notion for its organizational power, I realized what I need the most is quick access - for reading and writing. When I’m coding, my notes.md file is the way to go. For everything else I have a Google Doc in my bookmark that is just a single click away.

What makes Google Doc so convenient for me is because it is in the browser and is very light-weight.
I still need to create a different Google Doc for unrelated topics but the ease of access is unbeatable.
