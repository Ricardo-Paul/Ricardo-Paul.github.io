    2021 - What I’ve learned

    2022 is just around the corner, as always it feels good to get things off our chest after having gone through so much. When I set out to write this post, my head was immediately flooded with ideas and wasn’t sure what to begin with. A year is usually filled with experiences - good and bad ones- successes and failures. Throughout the year we also strive to improve different areas of our lives: our health, finance, education, career or others. So thoroughly summarizing the year would involve covering each of these areas with as many details as possible. I thought I would share a couple of lessons I’ve learned this year.

    Let’s dive straight in:

    Embrace uncertainty

    The year started out with uncertainties all over the place but maybe one of the most meaningful lessons I learned throughout these past soon-to-be-complete 12 months is to embrace uncertainty and try to grow out of it. Things are not always going to be as predictable as we wish they would, but with good faith, hard work and the right people around us we can still thrive.

    There’s no such thing as an easy way

    This lesson comes mostly from working as a Software Developer and putting in the work to level up but I think may be relevant across industries and in life in general.

    As a Software Developer, your goal sometimes may seem to be constantly learning complicated things and putting them to use. However, more often than I would like to admit, I fall into the trap of looking for the easy way: passively watching an easy tutorial which I think will give me the deep understanding that I need, reading 5-min articles hoping to achieve mastery. All that to try to avoid the heavy lifting which is maybe go and read the actual documentation and do the hard work of digging and thinking deeply.

    I’ve seen so many people around me who want the best in life but think they can actually cheat their way to success by looking for the easy path, well, what I’ve learned so far is it just doesn’t work or I should say unless you’re lucky.

    My 2021 in a nutshell

    1 - Better than 2020 when most of my plans were thwarted as Covid reared its ugly head.
    2 - I was able to bring some of my desires, dreams and wishes to fruition. So grateful !!!
