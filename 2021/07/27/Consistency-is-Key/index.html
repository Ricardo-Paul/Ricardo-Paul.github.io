Consistency is Key

Ricardo Paul, written on
2021-07-27
observation self-discipline

Oops! We are in July at the time of writing this blog, the last blog dates back to February, that’s quite a long time. Well, that’s part of the process of maintaining a blog, but that’s not what matters, it’s rather what has happened in between that matters the most. In between is a short journey of self-discovery where I learned many things about myself and this post is a result of it.

I woke up this morning at around 8 O’clock, think for a couple of minutes and then walk straight to my computer. I usually leave it on sleep mode throughout the night so it is just a matter of lifting the lid and enter my password before I can start knocking items off my todo list. I usually write that list the night before or early in the morning. While sitting at the computer about to start my day, I started to reflect about goal setting and consistency and how they relate to self-fulfillment, the conclusion that I came to is simple but may be difficult to grasp for people who haven’t tried it. I think the best way of feeling fulfilled is to stay true to yourself through consistency, it is to define a roadmap to achieve an end goal and stick to it.

I use to think that fulfillment is found somewhere else in the outside world, while that may be true to some extent, I think being goal-oriented and self-disciplined is the surest way to being fulfilled.
